## ACME CORPORATION
### MANAGEMENT'S DISCUSSION AND ANALYSIS OF FINANCIAL CONDITION AND RESULTS OF OPERATIONS

The following discussion should be read in conjunction with our Consolidated Financial Statements
and related Notes thereto. This discussion contains forward-looking statements that involve risks
and uncertainties. Actual results may differ materially from those anticipated.

---

## Overview

Fiscal year 2023 was another year of strong execution. Total net revenues grew 15.4% year-over-
year to $6.46 billion, driven by continued strength in our cloud-delivered product portfolio and
accelerating adoption of our professional services business. Net income reached $1.375 billion,
a 18.1% increase from fiscal 2022, reflecting our focus on operating leverage.

---

## Results of Operations

### Revenue

**Total net revenues** increased $863 million, or 15.4%, from $5.601 billion in 2022 to
$6.464 billion in 2023.

**Product revenue** increased $509 million, or 11.8%, from $4.312 billion to $4.821 billion.
Growth was driven primarily by:
- Cloud subscription ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue) expanding 24% to $3.2 billion
- Enterprise license renewal rates remaining stable at 94%
- Expansion into APAC and EMEA geographies, contributing approximately $180 million of incremental
  revenue

**Service revenue** increased $354 million, or 27.5%, from $1.289 billion to $1.643 billion.
The increase reflects robust demand for implementation and advisory services associated with
larger enterprise deployments, and a 34% increase in managed services contracts.

### Gross Profit

Gross profit increased $554 million, or 15.9%, from $3.490 billion to $4.044 billion.

Gross margin improved slightly from 62.3% to 62.6%, reflecting:
- Higher service gross margin (up 200 bps) due to workforce productivity improvements and
  automation in service delivery
- Partially offset by mix shift toward lower-margin managed services
- Product gross margin relatively stable at 60.0% vs. 60.0% in the prior year

### Operating Expenses

Total operating expenses increased $323 million, or 16.2%, from $1.989 billion to $2.312 billion.

**Research and development** expenses increased $111 million, or 15.8%, reflecting continued
investment in AI-enabled product features, platform security, and next-generation architecture.
R&D as a percentage of revenue was 12.6% in 2023 versus 12.5% in 2022.

**Sales and marketing** expenses increased $116 million, or 12.2%, reflecting headcount growth
and marketing programs supporting new product launches. Sales and marketing as a percentage of
revenue decreased from 17.0% to 16.5%, reflecting improved sales efficiency.

**General and administrative** expenses increased $51 million, or 15.2%, driven by higher
professional fees, compliance costs, and infrastructure to support company growth.

**Restructuring charges** of $45 million were recognized in 2023, related to workforce
optimization in our legacy on-premise product lines. We expect annualized savings of approximately
$60 million from this program.

### Operating Income

Operating income was $1.732 billion (operating margin 26.8%), compared to $1.501 billion
(operating margin 26.8%) in 2022. Operating income grew $231 million, or 15.4%, consistent
with revenue growth, demonstrating operating leverage at scale.

### Income Taxes

Our effective tax rate was 20.0% in 2023, compared to 19.0% in 2022. The increase reflects
(i) a reduced benefit from stock-based compensation deductions and (ii) changes in the geographic
mix of pre-tax income. We expect our 2024 effective tax rate to be in the range of 19-21%.

## Liquidity and Capital Resources

### Cash Flow Summary

|                                          | 2023    | 2022    |
|------------------------------------------|---------|---------|
| Net cash from operating activities       | $1,892  | $1,623  |
| Net cash used in investing activities    | ($487)  | ($412)  |
| Net cash used in financing activities    | ($1,120)| ($987)  |
| Net increase in cash                     | $285    | $224    |

**Operating cash flow** of $1.892 billion exceeded net income of $1.375 billion by $517 million,
reflecting strong cash conversion driven by working capital improvements (particularly deferred
revenue growth of $71 million) and non-cash charges including depreciation and stock compensation.

**Investing activities** of ($487 million) included capital expenditures of $312 million
(primarily data center infrastructure), strategic acquisitions of $98 million, and net purchases
of short-term investments of $77 million.

**Financing activities** of ($1.120 billion) reflected share repurchases of $875 million under
our Board-authorized $2.0 billion repurchase program, debt repayments of $145 million, and
dividend payments of $100 million.

### Capital Allocation

Our capital allocation priorities are: (1) invest in organic growth; (2) maintain investment-grade
credit profile (Net Debt / EBITDA below 1.5x); (3) pursue strategic M&A; (4) return excess
capital through repurchases and dividends.

As of December 31, 2023, we had $285 million remaining under our share repurchase authorization.

## Critical Accounting Estimates

### Revenue Recognition Under ASC 606
Determining standalone selling price (SSP) for bundled arrangements requires significant
judgment. Changes in SSP estimates could affect the timing and amount of revenue recognized.

### Goodwill Impairment
Goodwill of $2.341 billion is tested for impairment annually. A 100 basis point increase in the
weighted-average cost of capital (WACC) used in our discounted cash flow model would reduce
estimated fair value by approximately $340 million, which would not result in an impairment charge
based on current analysis.

### Income Taxes
Uncertain tax positions require judgment regarding the probability of sustaining those positions.
As of December 31, 2023, we have $87 million in unrecognized tax benefits. Unfavorable resolution
could increase our effective tax rate.
