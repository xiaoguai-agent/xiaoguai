## Statement of Commitment

[COMPANY NAME] is committed to the highest standards of ethical business conduct. This Code of
Business Conduct and Ethics ("Code") applies to all directors, officers, employees, and
contractors ("Covered Persons"). Compliance with this Code is a condition of employment or
engagement with the Company.

---

## 1. Integrity and Honesty

All Covered Persons are expected to act honestly and with integrity in all business dealings.
This includes:
- Making only truthful statements to colleagues, customers, suppliers, regulators, and the public
- Maintaining accurate and complete records in all business systems
- Not falsifying expense reports, timesheets, financial records, or any Company document
- Disclosing concerns promptly rather than concealing mistakes

---

## 2. Conflicts of Interest

A conflict of interest arises when personal interests interfere, or could interfere, with the
Company's interests. Covered Persons must:

- Avoid situations where personal financial interests, relationships, or outside activities
  could influence — or appear to influence — their judgment on behalf of the Company
- Disclose actual or potential conflicts to their manager and the Legal or HR department
- Obtain written approval before engaging in outside business activities that could compete
  with or create a conflict with the Company

**Examples of potential conflicts**:
- Investing in a supplier, customer, or competitor
- Hiring a family member into a role that reports to you or influences compensation decisions
- Accepting gifts exceeding $100 in fair market value from any single vendor or customer per year
- Directing Company business to a company in which you or a family member has a financial interest

---

## 3. Workplace Respect and Anti-Harassment

The Company is committed to a respectful workplace free from harassment and discrimination.

### Prohibited Conduct
Harassment or discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age,
disability, veteran status, sexual orientation, gender identity, or any other characteristic
protected by applicable law is prohibited.

Sexual harassment includes:
- Quid pro quo harassment (conditioning employment benefits on sexual favors)
- Hostile work environment harassment (severe or pervasive conduct of a sexual nature)

### Reporting
Employees who experience or witness harassment or discrimination must report it to HR, their
manager, or via the anonymous ethics hotline (details in Section 9).

### Non-Retaliation
Retaliation against anyone who reports a concern in good faith is strictly prohibited and is
itself a violation of this Code.

---

## 4. Confidential Information

### Company Confidential Information
All Covered Persons have an obligation to protect Company confidential information. This includes
trade secrets, business plans, financial data, customer lists, product roadmaps, and any
information that could harm the Company if disclosed. Confidential information may not be shared
externally without authorization and must be protected even after the end of employment or
engagement.

### Third-Party Confidential Information
Covered Persons must respect the confidentiality obligations of others. Do not solicit or accept
confidential information from third parties in a manner that would breach their obligations to
those parties.

---

## 5. Gifts and Entertainment

### Gifts from Third Parties
Covered Persons may not accept gifts worth more than $100 in fair market value from any single
vendor, customer, or other business partner in a calendar year. Gifts exceeding this limit must
be declined or returned, or the matter disclosed to and approved by the Legal department.

### Gifts to Third Parties
Gifts and entertainment extended to third parties must be:
- Reasonable in value and consistent with customary business practice
- Not intended to improperly influence a business decision
- Not cash or cash equivalents
- Permitted under the recipient's employer's policies and applicable law

### Government Officials
Special restrictions apply to gifts, meals, and entertainment involving government officials.
Consult Legal before any gift or entertainment involving a government employee.

---

## 6. Anti-Bribery and Corruption

The Company complies with the US Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA) and applicable anti-
bribery laws worldwide. No Covered Person may, directly or through third parties:
- Offer or pay bribes to government officials or commercial parties to obtain or retain business
- Make "facilitation payments" to expedite routine government actions
- Use a third-party intermediary to make payments that would be prohibited if made directly

---

## 7. Records Management and Data Privacy

### Accurate Records
All Company records must be accurate, complete, and maintained in accordance with applicable law
and Company record-retention schedules. Falsification of records is grounds for immediate
termination and potential legal liability.

### Data Privacy
Personal data of employees, customers, and other individuals must be handled in accordance with
applicable privacy laws and the Company's Privacy Policy. Covered Persons should access personal
data only as needed for legitimate business purposes.

---

## 8. Use of Company Assets

Company property and systems (including computers, network access, email, and software) are
provided for business use. Limited personal use is permitted but must not:
- Involve access to prohibited content (illegal material, offensive content)
- Interfere with job performance
- Expose Company systems to security risk

---

## 9. Reporting Violations and Ethics Hotline

Covered Persons who are aware of a potential violation of this Code, Company policy, or
applicable law must report it. Reporting options include:

- **Direct manager**: Appropriate for most workplace concerns
- **HR department**: For HR-related matters, harassment, or discrimination
- **Legal department**: For legal, compliance, financial, or regulatory concerns
- **Anonymous ethics hotline**: [PHONE NUMBER / URL] — available 24/7, anonymous reports accepted
- **Audit committee of the Board**: For concerns about financial fraud or officer misconduct

The Company prohibits retaliation against anyone who makes a good-faith report.

---

## 10. Consequences of Violations

Violations of this Code may result in disciplinary action up to and including immediate
termination of employment or engagement, and referral to law enforcement authorities where
appropriate.
