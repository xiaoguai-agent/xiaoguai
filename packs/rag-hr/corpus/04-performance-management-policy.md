## Purpose

This policy establishes the Company's performance management framework. The goal of performance
management is to align individual contributions with Company objectives, support professional
development, and provide a fair and transparent basis for compensation and advancement decisions.

---

## Annual Review Cycle

The Company conducts formal performance reviews on an annual basis, with a mid-year check-in.

| Review Milestone | Timing | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Goal Setting | January (Q1) | Establish SMART goals for the year |
| Mid-Year Check-In | July (Q3) | Progress review, goal adjustment, coaching |
| Annual Review | November–December | Rating assignment, development planning |
| Compensation Calibration | December–January | Merit increases, bonus recommendations |

### Goals

All employees are expected to have 3–5 SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant,
Time-bound) goals documented in the performance management system by the end of Q1. Goals
should be:
- Aligned with team and Company OKRs
- Reviewed and approved by the direct manager
- Updated with progress notes at each check-in

## Performance Ratings

The Company uses a five-point performance rating scale:

| Rating | Label | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Exceptional | Consistently exceeds expectations; top-quartile impact; role model for peers |
| 4 | Exceeds Expectations | Regularly exceeds most goals; high impact and quality |
| 3 | Meets Expectations | Consistently meets all goals; solid, reliable contributor |
| 2 | Partially Meets Expectations | Meets some goals; performance improvement needed in key areas |
| 1 | Does Not Meet Expectations | Fails to meet most goals; immediate improvement plan required |

**Calibration**: All ratings are subject to calibration by department leadership to ensure
consistency and equity across teams. Individual managers may not finalize ratings without
calibration approval.

**Distribution guidelines**: The Company does not enforce a forced distribution. However,
managers are expected to differentiate performance meaningfully. Departments with more than
50% of employees rated "Exceptional" or "Exceeds" will be flagged for calibration review.

## Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

### When a PIP Is Used
A Performance Improvement Plan is initiated when an employee receives a rating of 1 (Does Not
Meet Expectations) or when an employee's performance is declining significantly between review
cycles.

### PIP Structure
A PIP must include:
- Specific, measurable performance deficiencies with examples
- Clear performance expectations and measurable milestones
- Timeline: typically 30, 60, or 90 days depending on the nature of the deficiencies
- Support resources provided by the Company (training, coaching, additional check-ins)
- Consequences of failing to meet PIP expectations (up to and including termination)

### PIP Process
1. Manager documents performance deficiencies with HR support
2. PIP document reviewed and approved by HR and manager's manager
3. PIP presented to employee in a formal meeting; employee acknowledges receipt in writing
4. Weekly check-ins between manager and employee during PIP period
5. Formal PIP outcome documented at end of PIP period

### PIP Outcomes
- **Successful completion**: Employee removed from PIP; may return to standard review process.
  Continued performance monitoring for 6 months.
- **Unsuccessful completion**: Employment may be terminated.

## Compensation Link

### Merit Increases
Merit increases are awarded annually following the performance review cycle and are subject to
budget approval. Target merit increase ranges by rating:

| Rating | Typical Merit Increase Range |
|---|---|
| Exceptional (5) | 6–10% |
| Exceeds Expectations (4) | 4–7% |
| Meets Expectations (3) | 2–4% |
| Partially Meets (2) | 0–1% |
| Does Not Meet (1) | 0% |

These ranges are guidelines only; actual increases depend on budget, position-in-range, and
calibration.

### Bonus
Bonus recommendations are linked to performance ratings:

| Rating | Bonus Payout (% of Target) |
|---|---|
| Exceptional (5) | 120–150% |
| Exceeds Expectations (4) | 100–120% |
| Meets Expectations (3) | 75–100% |
| Partially Meets (2) | 0–50% |
| Does Not Meet (1) | 0% |

Bonus is also subject to overall Company financial performance against plan.

---

## Documentation and Record Retention

All performance review records, including goal documents, mid-year check-ins, annual ratings,
PIP documents, and calibration notes, are retained in the performance management system for a
minimum of 7 years following the employee's termination date, or as required by applicable law.

---

## Appeals

Employees who believe their performance rating was unfair may appeal by submitting a written
appeal to HR within 14 calendar days of receiving the final rating. HR will review the appeal
with input from the employee, manager, and skip-level, and issue a written decision within
21 calendar days.
