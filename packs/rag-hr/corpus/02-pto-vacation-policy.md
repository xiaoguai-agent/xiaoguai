## Policy Statement

[COMPANY NAME] provides a Paid Time Off (PTO) benefit to full-time and part-time employees. PTO
encompasses time off for vacation, personal needs, and minor illness, providing flexibility to
employees in managing their work-life balance. PTO does not accrue separately from vacation or
sick leave — it is a unified bank.

## Eligibility

- **Full-time employees** (30+ hours/week): Eligible from the first day of employment; accrual
  begins on day one.
- **Part-time employees** (20-29 hours/week): Eligible for PTO at a prorated rate based on
  scheduled weekly hours.
- **Temporary and contract employees**: Not eligible for PTO.

## Accrual Schedule

PTO accrues each pay period (bi-weekly). Accrual rates increase with tenure:

| Years of Service | Annual PTO Accrual | Per Pay Period Accrual |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 2 years | 15 days (120 hours) | 4.62 hours |
| 2 – 5 years | 18 days (144 hours) | 5.54 hours |
| 5 – 10 years | 22 days (176 hours) | 6.77 hours |
| 10+ years | 25 days (200 hours) | 7.69 hours |

Part-time employees accrue PTO at (Scheduled Hours / 40) × full-time accrual rate per pay period.

**Accrual during leave**: PTO accrues during paid leave (including FMLA leave substituted with
PTO). PTO does not accrue during unpaid leave of absence.

## Maximum Balance (Accrual Cap)

To encourage employees to use PTO, the maximum PTO balance is capped at **1.5 × annual
accrual** for the employee's tenure band. Once the cap is reached, no further PTO accrues
until the balance falls below the cap.

| Years of Service | Cap (Hours) |
|---|---|
| 0 – 2 years | 180 hours |
| 2 – 5 years | 216 hours |
| 5 – 10 years | 264 hours |
| 10+ years | 300 hours |

## Carryover

- **Standard carryover**: Unused PTO may be carried over from one calendar year to the next,
  up to the accrual cap. PTO in excess of the cap is forfeited on January 1.
- **No rollover extension** for employees on approved leave of absence. The same cap applies.
- **State exceptions**: In states where "use-it-or-lose-it" policies are prohibited (e.g.,
  California, Colorado), PTO is treated as vested wages and does not expire. The Company will
  administer state-specific rules where required.

## Using PTO

- **Minimum increment**: PTO may be taken in increments of no less than 1 hour.
- **Advance notice**: Employees should provide at least 5 business days' advance notice for
  planned PTO of 3 or more consecutive days. Emergency time off should be reported as soon
  as practicable.
- **Manager approval**: All PTO requests are subject to manager approval based on business needs.
  The Company will not unreasonably deny PTO requests.
- **During FMLA leave**: PTO may be (and for most qualifying reasons, must be) substituted
  concurrently with FMLA leave.

## PTO Payout on Termination

- **Voluntary resignation or involuntary termination**: Accrued, unused PTO is **paid out** at
  the employee's regular hourly rate on the final paycheck, subject to applicable state law.
  *Exception*: Employees who resign without providing required notice (per company policy) may
  forfeit payout of accrued PTO not to exceed 40 hours, where permitted by state law.
- **Death**: Accrued PTO is paid to the employee's estate or designated beneficiary.

## Leave Balance Computation Example

**Scenario**: An employee has 3 years of tenure (accrual rate: 5.54 hours/pay period). They
have taken 5 days (40 hours) of PTO so far this calendar year and have been employed for 16
pay periods since their last anniversary.

- Accrued this year: 16 × 5.54 = 88.64 hours
- Used this year: 40 hours
- **Current balance**: 88.64 - 40 = **48.64 hours** (approximately 6 days)
- Balance at prior year end (carried over): assume 100 hours
- **Total current balance**: 100 + 48.64 = 148.64 hours
- **Cap for this tenure band**: 216 hours — well below cap, accrual continues.
