## Policy Statement

[COMPANY NAME] ("Company") complies with the Family and Medical Leave Act of 1993 (FMLA) and
applicable state family and medical leave laws. This policy describes your rights and obligations
under FMLA. The Company will not interfere with, restrain, or deny the exercise of any right
provided under this policy, and will not discharge or discriminate against any individual for
exercising FMLA rights.

## Eligibility

An employee is eligible for FMLA leave if all of the following conditions are met:

1. **Employed by the Company for at least 12 months** (need not be consecutive; breaks in
   service of seven or more years due to military service obligations do not count toward the
   12-month requirement).

2. **Worked at least 1,250 hours during the 12-month period** immediately preceding the start
   of leave. Part-time employees' hours are counted; overtime hours count.

3. **Works at a location where the Company employs 50 or more employees within 75 miles**.

## Qualifying Reasons for FMLA Leave

An eligible employee may take FMLA leave for the following reasons:

1. **Birth of a child**: To care for a newborn child within the first 12 months of birth.

2. **Adoption / foster placement**: For the placement of a child with the employee for adoption
   or foster care, within the first 12 months after placement.

3. **Serious health condition — immediate family member**: To care for the employee's spouse,
   child, or parent with a serious health condition.

4. **Employee's own serious health condition**: When the employee is unable to perform one or
   more essential functions of their job due to a serious health condition.

5. **Qualifying military exigency**: For qualifying exigency arising from the employee's spouse,
   child, or parent being on covered active duty or called to covered active duty.

6. **Military caregiver leave**: To care for a covered servicemember with a serious injury or
   illness. Up to 26 workweeks per single 12-month period.

A **serious health condition** means an illness, injury, impairment, or physical or mental
condition that involves: (a) inpatient care; or (b) continuing treatment by a healthcare provider
(generally, incapacity for more than 3 consecutive full calendar days plus continuing treatment
by a healthcare provider).

## Duration of Leave

- **Standard FMLA leave**: Up to **12 workweeks** per 12-month period for reasons 1–5 above.
- **Military caregiver leave**: Up to **26 workweeks** per single 12-month period.
- The 12-month period is measured as a rolling 12-month period measured backward from the date
  an employee uses FMLA leave ("rolling backward method").
- Intermittent or reduced-schedule leave may be taken for qualifying reasons.

## Substitution of Paid Leave

FMLA leave is unpaid unless applicable law or Company policy requires otherwise. The Company will
require employees to use accrued paid time off (PTO/vacation) and, if applicable, accrued sick
leave concurrently with FMLA leave. Use of paid leave runs concurrently with FMLA leave and does
not extend the 12-week entitlement.

## Benefits Continuation

During FMLA leave, the Company will maintain the employee's group health insurance coverage on
the same terms as if the employee had continued to work. Employees must continue to pay their
share of premiums during leave. If the employee does not return from FMLA leave, the Company may
recover its share of health plan premiums paid during leave, with limited exceptions.

## Notification Requirements

### Employee Notice
- **Foreseeable leave**: At least 30 days' advance notice. If 30 days is not practicable,
  notice must be given as soon as practicable (generally the same or next business day).
- **Unforeseeable leave**: Notice must be given as soon as practicable. An employee generally
  must follow the Company's call-in procedures unless unable to do so.

### Medical Certification
The Company may require a completed medical certification form from a healthcare provider,
typically within 15 calendar days of the request.

## Restoration Rights

Upon return from FMLA leave, the employee is entitled to the same position or an equivalent
position with equivalent pay, benefits, and other terms and conditions of employment.

A "key employee" exception applies in limited circumstances: reinstatement may be denied for
certain highly compensated salaried employees if reinstatement would cause substantial and
grievous economic injury to the Company's operations.

## State Law

This policy is a minimum standard. Where applicable state law provides greater leave rights,
state law governs. Employees should contact HR for information about state-specific rights.
