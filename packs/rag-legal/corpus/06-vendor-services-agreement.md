VENDOR SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Vendor Services Agreement ("Agreement") is made between [CLIENT NAME] ("Client") and
[VENDOR NAME] ("Vendor") as of [DATE] ("Effective Date").

## 1. Services and Statements of Work

### 1.1 Statements of Work
Vendor will perform services ("Services") as described in one or more Statements of Work
("SOWs") executed by both Parties. Each SOW will specify: (a) a description of the Services;
(b) deliverables and acceptance criteria; (c) project timeline and milestones; (d) fees and
payment schedule; (e) any special terms applicable to that SOW.

### 1.2 Conflicts
In the event of a conflict between this Agreement and an SOW, the SOW controls unless it
expressly states otherwise.

### 1.3 Change Orders
Either Party may request changes to an SOW by submitting a written change request. No change
to an SOW is binding unless signed by authorized representatives of both Parties.

## 2. Acceptance and Rejection

### 2.1 Acceptance Testing
Upon delivery of each deliverable, Client has fifteen (15) business days to review and either:
(a) accept the deliverable in writing; or
(b) reject the deliverable in a written notice that identifies, in reasonable detail, the defects
    or deficiencies that prevent acceptance ("Deficiency Notice").

### 2.2 Cure Period
Vendor shall have fifteen (15) business days after receiving a Deficiency Notice to correct the
identified deficiencies and re-deliver. Client will then have a further ten (10) business day
period to review.

### 2.3 Deemed Acceptance
If Client does not provide written acceptance or rejection within the applicable review period,
the deliverable is deemed accepted.

## 3. Fees and Payment

### 3.1 Fees
Client will pay Vendor the fees set forth in each SOW. Unless otherwise stated, invoices are due
within thirty (30) days of receipt.

### 3.2 Expense Reimbursement
Client will reimburse Vendor for pre-approved, documented, reasonable out-of-pocket expenses.

### 3.3 Disputed Invoices
Client may dispute an invoice in good faith by providing written notice within 10 days of receipt
describing the dispute. The Parties will work in good faith to resolve disputes within 30 days.
Undisputed amounts remain due on the original schedule.

## 4. Intellectual Property

### 4.1 Work Product Ownership
Subject to full payment of all Fees, Vendor hereby assigns to Client all right, title, and
interest in and to all deliverables and work product created specifically for Client under this
Agreement ("Work Product"), including all copyrights, patents, and other intellectual property
rights therein, to the extent such Work Product constitutes a "work made for hire" or, if not,
by assignment.

### 4.2 Vendor Background IP
Vendor retains all rights to pre-existing tools, methodologies, know-how, and background
intellectual property ("Background IP") used in performing the Services. Vendor grants Client a
perpetual, royalty-free, non-exclusive license to use Background IP solely to the extent
incorporated in the Work Product and necessary for Client to use the Work Product as intended.

### 4.3 Third-Party Components
Vendor will disclose to Client any third-party open-source or proprietary components incorporated
into deliverables, including applicable license terms, prior to delivery.

## 5. Confidentiality

Each Party will maintain the confidentiality of the other's Confidential Information and use it
only to perform obligations under this Agreement. "Confidential Information" excludes information
that is publicly known, independently developed, or received from a third party without restriction.

## 6. Warranties

Vendor warrants that:
(a) it has the right and authority to enter into this Agreement and perform the Services;
(b) the Services will be performed in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with
    industry standards;
(c) the deliverables will materially conform to the specifications in the applicable SOW for
    sixty (60) days after acceptance;
(d) to Vendor's knowledge, the deliverables will not infringe any third-party intellectual
    property rights.

## 7. Indemnification

### 7.1 By Vendor
Vendor will defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Client from third-party claims that any
deliverable infringes a third-party intellectual property right, provided Client promptly notifies
Vendor of the claim and cooperates in the defense.

### 7.2 By Client
Client will defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Vendor from third-party claims arising from
Client's use of the deliverables in violation of applicable law or this Agreement.

## 8. Limitation of Liability

NEITHER PARTY IS LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, OR CONSEQUENTIAL DAMAGES.
EACH PARTY'S MAXIMUM AGGREGATE LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT IS LIMITED TO THE FEES PAID OR
PAYABLE TO VENDOR IN THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM, EXCEPT THAT THE CAP DOES NOT
APPLY TO A PARTY'S INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS OR GROSS NEGLIGENCE OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT.

## 9. Term and Termination

### 9.1 Term
This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues until terminated.

### 9.2 Termination for Convenience
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW on sixty (60) days' written notice.

### 9.3 Termination for Cause
Either Party may terminate immediately upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches
this Agreement and fails to cure within thirty (30) days of written notice.

### 9.4 Effect of Termination
Client will pay Vendor for all Services satisfactorily performed through the termination date.
Sections 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, and 10 survive termination.

## 10. Governing Law and Disputes

This Agreement is governed by the laws of [STATE]. Disputes will be resolved by binding
arbitration in [CITY, STATE] under AAA Commercial Arbitration Rules. The prevailing Party in
any arbitration is entitled to recover its reasonable attorneys' fees and costs.
